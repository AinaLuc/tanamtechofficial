EIN for Non-Resident Business Owners: A Comprehensive Guide

Understanding and obtaining an Employer Identification Number (EIN) is a critical step for non-resident business owners looking to operate in the United States. This guide provides essential information on what EIN is, its importance, and how non-residents can apply for it.

What is an EIN?

The Employer Identification Number (EIN) is a nine-digit number assigned by the IRS to business entities for tax identification purposes. It's essential for hiring employees, opening business bank accounts, and more.

Uses of an EIN

An EIN is used to identify a business entity and is necessary for a variety of business activities including tax filing and compliance with IRS. It's a must-have for non-residents who wish to conduct business in the U.S.

How Non-Residents Can Obtain an EIN

Non-residents without a Social Security Number (SSN) can still obtain an EIN by completing Form SS-4 and either mailing or faxing it to the IRS. It's also possible to apply by phone if preferred. Detailed instructions are available on the IRS website.
