Insufficient Documentation

One of the most common reasons for E-2 visa refusal is the lack of adequate documentation to demonstrate the investment, business plan, or the essential skills of the applicant. Ensuring thorough and relevant documentation is key to a successful application.

Investment Not Considered Substantial

An E-2 visa requires a "substantial" investment. Refusals often occur when the consular officer deems the investment insufficient to ensure the successful operation of the business. Detailed financial plans and evidence of investment can help mitigate this issue.

Investment in a Marginal Enterprise

Investments in businesses that do not have the capacity to generate significantly more income than just to provide a living for the applicant and their family are considered marginal. Demonstrating growth potential and employment creation is crucial.

Unclear Intent to Depart

Applicants must demonstrate an unequivocal intent to depart the U.S. once their E-2 status ends. Lack of clear ties to their home country can lead to visa refusal.
